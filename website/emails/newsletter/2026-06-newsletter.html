UBI Stunts

It's rare to hear the winners of the AI revolution argue for taxing themselves and handing the proceeds to everyone else – but that's exactly what OpenAI just did. Publicity stunt? Possibly. They've never been first in line to be regulated. Still, it opens a debate that isn't going away.

Loud Truth

Very few people get to call out what's broken and actually be heard. Tom Fletcher, the UN's humanitarian chief, is one of them - and his Chatham House talk doesn't waste a word. If you care about where the world is heading, this one's worth your full attention.

He also believes in cash transfers. Consider this a warm endorsement.

Friedman's Ghost

“The government isn't responsible for solving poverty. People are. Markets are.”

This statement from Milton Friedman split the world in two. Forty years later, both sides have a point — the market did lift millions out of poverty, and it built the most extreme inequality in history at the same time.

Turns out poverty doesn't care about ideology — it needs markets, people, and governments all showing up. And sometimes a crisis, as Milton also said.

My World in Data

37.5%

A CALP Network analysis combed through $11.4 billion in aid across more than 1,200 programs and landed on an uncomfortable number: cash assistance is 37.5% more cost-efficient to deliver than in-kind food aid. That's not a marginal gain. It's the difference between a system designed around logistics and one designed around people.

The world's 200 million migrants figured this out long ago. They call it remittances. As you’ll know if you’ve read my article.

€220

That's what Sub-Saharan Africa spent per child on education in 2025. Europe spent €7,400. North America €9,000. Two children, same dream. One has €220 to get there. The other has €9,000 — 40 times more, just for being born elsewhere. Play the birth lottery here.

The €220 problem has a partial answer. 68% of Social Income recipients say they spend it on education.
