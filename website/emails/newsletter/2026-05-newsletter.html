Field Notes

Milestones turn good intentions into deadlines. We actually like them. 500 recipients by June 2026, set by our board. 1,000 by April 2027, set with our platform partner Somaha. Both within reach, I would say. One essential part of it is finding the right NGO local partners. Here's where we stand:

  • In Sierra Leone 🇸🇱, we're up to 10 local NGO partners. Jessica is writing up the two newest — they'll be on the partner page soon.

  • In Liberia 🇱🇷, we're moving from pilot to long-term program for young mothers. One income, two lives. In partnership with the local Help a Mother and Newborn Initiative.

  • In Ghana 🇬🇭, we are exploring a regular program for young adults who lost their footing, landed on the streets, and decided that wasn't the end of the story. Together with “Chance for Children”.

 

Last impression

As we expanded into Liberia and Ghana, our stories are growing too. Alongside updates from Sierra Leone, you’ll now get glimpses from these two new chapters - starting with something unexpected: Ghana’s extraordinary handcrafted coffins, designed to reflect a person’s life, passions, or profession. Equal parts tribute and artistry, they turn farewells into something vividly personal.

A final resting place, yes - but arguably the most creative exit strategy around.

Gap fix

Ending extreme poverty might be less about miracles and more about math. A recent Economist piece argues that instead of waiting for entire economies to boom, simply topping up incomes – closing the gap between what people have and what they need – could do the job for a surprisingly small share of global GDP (0.3%!). In that light, ideas like UBI stop sounding radical and start looking… practical.

Turns out, ending poverty might just be a rounding error - if we choose to round up.

My World in Data

2000

In 1995, Anguilla was assigned the domain .ai – ignored for decades until ChatGPT and the AI boom turned it into a goldmine. Today, 2000 new .ai domains are registered daily, bringing in millions and funding half of the island’s budget, from tax cuts to new public services and even an international airport.

Anguilla didn't build AI. It just happened to spell it.

800,000,000

Last year, X removed around 800 million accounts in an effortto curb state-backed manipulation. This is nearly three times its roughly 300 million monthly active users. A reminder that not all “engagement” is created equal.
