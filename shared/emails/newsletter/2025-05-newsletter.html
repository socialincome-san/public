Moral ambition

Rutger Bregman has been a quiet compass behind many of the thoughts shared in this newsletter. You might know him from Utopia for Realists, one of our must-reads. His new book, Moral Ambition, just came out. I haven’t dived in yet, but this excerpt from The Guardian really stuck with me and maybe it will for you too:

Moral ambition begins with a simple realisation: you’ve only got one life. The time you have left on this Earth is your most precious possession. You can’t buy yourself more time, and every hour you’ve spent is gone forever.

Rutger Bregman

Green peace

I feel at peace whenever I go for a walk in the forest. As turns out, Sierra Leone and Liberia feel the same. The two countries are joining forces to protect the Gola Rainforest through a $4 million UN-backed project that blends conservation with peacebuilding.

It’s not just about green spaces — it’s about common ground.

Age hacks

Roger Rosenblatt’s tips for being happy at the age of 85 made me pause. I’m nowhere near that age, but tips like “nobody’s thinking about you,” “make young friends,” and “join a gang” felt surprisingly relevant. But what if it’s the wrong gang? The wrong friends? …

He’s got a point about regrets too: They’re part of life. Learn to live with them.

Happy money

Another month, another UBI experiment that you can use as an argument for our work. In Germany, recipients received €1,200 a month with no strings attached. They didn’t work less, but they slept better, stressed less, and even switched careers with confidence.

Give people money, and they don’t do less — they do more.

My World in Data

27

April 27 is Sierra Leone Independence Day. On 27 April 1961, Sir Milton Margai led Sierra Leone to independence from Great Britain and became the country's first prime minister.

In case you missed the party, here’s how long you have to wait for the next one.

0.1%

Despite accounting for 19% of the world’s population, Africa supplies just 0.1% of global computing capacity – leaving most of its AI talent unable to train tomorrow’s large language models. Today, only 5% of Africa’s AI talent has access to the computational power needed for complex tasks.

Africa has the talent. Now it just needs the servers.

1,400,000,000

In 2021 and 2022, the EU allocated nearly $1.4 billion to humanitarian aid through cash transfers—24.6% of its total aid budget. It’s a big step toward trusting people with direct support. The momentum is strong for now — but with US government funding pulling back by 2025, the overall amount is likely to fall.
