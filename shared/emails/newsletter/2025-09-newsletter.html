Big news: We were one of 26 successful NGO applicants for funding (out of 1,500) from the European Union! We will soon be supporting farmers living in absolute poverty in Sierra Leone.

Now onto other news.

Bold beginnings

We can plan forever and never make a start. Instead, we’re beginning now in Liberia with a small cash transfer pilot, working with volunteers in rural libraries who both benefit from support and help design the program.

This pilot isn’t just theory – it’s happening.

Wealthy roses

The newest trend in Silicon Valley envisions a future in which AI generates a surge of new wealth that can be shared amongst the masses. Sam Altman, Elon Musk and others predict that the revenue generated by AI could make us all wealthier.

I just have one question: Why are all these billionaires investing millions in doomsday bunkers when they predict such a rosy future?

What went wrong?

The pioneers of the digital revolution wanted barrier-free education for all. We got TikTok and fake news instead. They wanted global connection and experimental play. We got surveillance and data exploitation.

I borrowed this introduction from a brilliant talk by Douglas Rushkoff, and figured it would make a great opening for this email, but then the news about the EU funding came in. His observations really nail what’s happening right now. About the AI dudes mentioned above, he says:

For them, the future of technology is about only one thing: escape from the rest of us.

Suspicious maps

Back in high school, I wasted hours hunting for quirky websites. The True Size of… quickly became a favorite. It blows my mind how the Mercator map tricks our conception of country sizes, like making Russia look way bigger than Africa.

This made me realize that the longer we use a tool, the less we question it. By the way, the African Union wants the world to stop using the 450-year-old distorted world map.

Conversation starters

When small talk hits a dead end, I pull out my secret weapon: weirdly fascinating websites you won’t stumble upon in your average Google search. So, in the spirit of not gatekeeping, here are three of my all-time favorites:

  • Lightning Maps shows you all the lightning that strikes the worldin real time. Use it when you’re in a storm and you will be amazed at the accuracy.

  • Draw a Fish evaluates the accuracy of the fish that you draw and even lets it swim.

  • Climate Pulse gives you real-time insights into major climate and geological events worldwide.

 

My World in Data

1 doctor

In January 2025, Sierra Leone got its first, and only, neurosurgeon. This means one doctor that can diagnose and treat conditions affecting the brain, spinal cord and nerves throughout the body. Before, patients’ only chance was to go to another country to be treated “if you are lucky and rich”.

2 trillion

In 2024, the world’s billionaires grew richer by two trillion dollars. This small tip was added to their fortunes in a single year, at a pace three times faster than before. Meanwhile, nearly half the world’s population still survives on less than seven dollars a day.

3 dollars

Jason Hickel tirelessly advocated for a higher poverty line. In June 2025, the World Bank updated its global poverty line to US $3 a day. What changed, and why? This increased the global extreme poverty count by 125 million people. However, critics argue that this is still an extremely low threshold which defines a level of income much lower than just the cost of a healthy diet.
