Birth lottery

One of the most widely used measures of development is the Human Development Index (HDI), published by the UN. Unsurprisingly, rich countries rank highest and handled COVID-19 best. The HDI offers concrete insights:

  • If I were to have a baby 👶🏼 this year in Switzerland, it is expected to live 84 years, attend school for 16 years, and grow up with an average income of $82,000.
    Quick disclaimer for my mum and boyfriend reading along: This is just a thought experiment. Last time I checked, there was no baby on the way.

  • A baby born in the US can expect to live 79 years, attend school for 16 years, and grow up with an average income of $74,000.

  • A baby born in Sierra Leone can expect to live 62 years, attend school for 9 years, and grow up with an average income of $1,700.

Pink okada

If you’ve ever been to Sierra Leone, you’ve seen the okadas – motorbikes zipping through traffic on two wheels. For many, they’re the only affordable way to reach a clinic, market or workplace. Riding one has long meant a job only for men.

Now there is also Mariama Timbo and her students.
