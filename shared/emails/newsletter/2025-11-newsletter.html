Academic romance

You know you’ve spent too long on your thesis when your sources start feeling like friends. So imagine my thrill when I learned Esther Duflo and Abhijit Banerjee, the Nobel-winning power couple of development economics, are coming to the University of Zurich. This my academic fangirl moment and chance to share three of their most myth-busting insights:

  • Cash boosts last a long time: Giving cash doesn’t just help people in poverty to survive, it helps them thrive, fueling better jobs and higher incomes for years.

  • Cash works smarter: When people get no-strings cash, they spend it on school and food, not vices, busting the myth that the poor “waste” money.

  • Poverty isn’t destiny: It’s about missed chances, not laziness. Cash transfers can turn things around.

Emotional weight
